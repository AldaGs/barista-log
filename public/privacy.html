Slurry Stats — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 25 June 2026

Slurry Stats (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a coffee-brewing logbook. This policy explains what data the app handles and, importantly, what it does not do. Slurry Stats is a local-first application: your data is created and stored on your own device, and we do not operate a server that collects or stores it.

1. Data you create

Beans, recipes, brew sessions, gear, grinders, water profiles, your profile, and app settings are stored locally on your device using your browser’s storage (IndexedDB and local storage). This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to back it up (see below). We do not have access to it.

2. Optional Google Drive backup

Google Drive backup is an opt-in feature you enable from the app’s Settings. When enabled, Slurry Stats interacts with Google user data as described below.

2.1 Google user data the app accesses

The app requests exactly one OAuth scope: https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.appdata. This scope restricts the app to a hidden, application-specific data folder in your Google Drive. With this scope the app can read and write only the backup file it creates in that folder and cannot see, read, list, modify, or delete any other file in your Google Drive. The app does not request access to your Gmail, Calendar, Contacts, Photos, profile information, or any other Google service.

The contents of the backup file are the local app data you have created in Slurry Stats — beans, recipes, brew sessions, gear, grinders, water profiles, your profile, app settings, and preferences — serialized as a single JSON file.

2.2 How the app uses this Google user data

  • Purpose: the sole purpose of accessing Google user data is to let you back up your Slurry Stats data to, and restore it from, your own Google Drive, so you can recover it or move it between devices.
  • Use of the backup file: the app writes your local data to the backup file when you create or update a backup, and reads it back when you restore. The file is overwritten on each backup.
  • No server-side processing: all reads and writes happen directly between your device and Google’s servers. We do not operate a backend that receives, processes, or proxies your Google user data.
  • No secondary use: we do not use Google user data to train AI/ML models, for advertising, for analytics, for profiling, or for any purpose other than the backup/restore feature described here.

2.3 How the app stores and shares Google user data

  • Storage of the backup file: the backup file is stored in your Google Drive’s hidden app-data folder. We do not store a copy on any server we control.
  • Tokens and credentials: authorization uses Google Identity Services. The OAuth access token is held only in your browser’s memory for the duration of a session, is never sent to or stored on any server we control, and is discarded when the session ends. We do not request or receive a refresh token and retain no long-lived Google credentials.
  • Sharing: we do not transfer, sell, share, or disclose Google user data to any third party. The backup file moves directly from your device to your Google Drive.

3. Google API Services User Data Policy

Slurry Stats’ use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We do not use Google user data for advertising, and we do not sell or share it.

4. What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone.
  • We do not use your data for advertising or profiling.
  • We do not run analytics or tracking that collects your brewing data.
  • We do not store your data on servers we operate.

5. Revoking access and deleting your data

  • You can disconnect Google Drive at any time in the app’s Settings.
  • You can delete the backup file from your Drive using “Remove backup from Drive” in Settings, or manually from your Google account.
  • You can revoke the app’s access entirely at your Google Account permissions page.
  • Clearing the app’s site data in your browser removes all locally stored data.

6. Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect their data.

7. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
