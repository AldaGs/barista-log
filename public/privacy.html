Slurry Stats — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 23 June 2026

Slurry Stats (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a coffee-brewing logbook. This policy explains what data the app handles and, importantly, what it does not do. Slurry Stats is a local-first application: your data is created and stored on your own device, and we do not operate a server that collects or stores it.

1. Data you create

Beans, recipes, brew sessions, gear, grinders, water profiles, your profile, and app settings are stored locally on your device using your browser’s storage (IndexedDB and local storage). This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly choose to back it up (see below). We do not have access to it.

2. Optional Google Drive backup

If you choose to connect Google Drive, the app backs up a single JSON file to your own Google Drive account. We request only the https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.appdata scope, which grants access to a private, app-specific hidden folder in your Drive. With this scope the app:

  • can read and write only the backup file it creates in that hidden folder;
  • cannot see, read, or access any of your other Google Drive files;
  • does not transmit your data to us or to any third party — the file goes from your device directly to your Google Drive.

Authorization uses Google Identity Services. The access token is held only in your browser’s memory for the duration of a session and is never sent to or stored on any server we control. We do not receive a refresh token and retain no Google credentials.

3. Google API Services User Data Policy

Slurry Stats’ use and transfer of information received from Google APIs adheres to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements. We do not use Google user data for advertising, and we do not sell or share it.

4. What we do NOT do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your data with anyone.
  • We do not use your data for advertising or profiling.
  • We do not run analytics or tracking that collects your brewing data.
  • We do not store your data on servers we operate.

5. Revoking access and deleting your data

  • You can disconnect Google Drive at any time in the app’s Settings.
  • You can delete the backup file from your Drive using “Remove backup from Drive” in Settings, or manually from your Google account.
  • You can revoke the app’s access entirely at your Google Account permissions page.
  • Clearing the app’s site data in your browser removes all locally stored data.

6. Children

The app is not directed to children under 13 and does not knowingly collect their data.

7. Changes

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be reflected by the “Last updated” date above.
